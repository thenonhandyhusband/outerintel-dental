The Finding

Stars are table stakes. The race has moved on.

Sixty-nine percent of independent med spas in the US average 4.9 stars or higher. The top quartile and the bottom quartile of this study are nearly identical on star rating (4.93 vs. 4.87), sentiment, and review specificity. They are dramatically different on one thing: whether the owner shows up to manage the practice's review record at all.

The businesses winning in AI search are not better practices. They are more operationally present ones. Zero businesses in the top quartile respond to 0% of their reviews. Seven businesses in the bottom quartile do. That is the finding.

A business can rank in the top 20 nationally while its reputation is quietly deteriorating.

One business in this study holds a 4.5 star rating and ranks top 20 on composite AI Visibility Score. Its sentiment score is the lowest in the study at 6.1 out of 10, and its negative review rate is 12%, nearly six times the national mean of 2.1%. Star ratings mask what AI engines actually read.
